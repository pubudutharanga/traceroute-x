## Wait — Razr 70 Ultra or Razr Ultra 2026?

Let's clear up the naming confusion before we go any further, because Motorola loves to keep things interesting.

Outside the United States, this phone is marketed as the **Motorola Razr 70 Ultra**. In the US — and on the shelves of every major American carrier — it will land simply as the **Motorola Razr Ultra (2026)**. Same phone, different badge. Motorola has followed this pattern for several years now, so don't let the dual identity throw you off.

Throughout this article we'll use both names interchangeably, just like the rest of the tech world does.

## Displays: A Double-Screen Dream

The Razr 70 Ultra's dual-display setup is where it truly separates itself from the competition.

### The Outer 4-Inch Cover Screen

This is Motorola's ace in the hole. While Samsung's Galaxy Z Flip 7 reportedly sticks with a small, limited cover display, Motorola's **4-inch, 1080×1272 AMOLED panel** is essentially a fully functional mini-phone in its own right.

You can run nearly any app — Instagram, Google Maps, Spotify, your email — without ever unfolding the device. It's the kind of feature that sounds gimmicky until you use it for a week and can't imagine going back.

### The 7-Inch Inner Foldable Display

Open the clamshell and you're greeted by a sweeping **7-inch AMOLED display** running at a crisp 2992×1224 resolution. The 120Hz refresh rate keeps scrolling and gaming silky smooth, and Motorola's engineers have worked hard to minimize the center crease — something reviewers of the 2025 model praised highly.

### What Moto AI Does for Your Photos

The Razr 70 Ultra's camera system is tightly integrated with **Moto AI**, Motorola's suite of on-device and cloud-powered intelligence. Here's what that means in practice:

- **Signature Style** — Train the AI on your favorite shots, and it learns your aesthetic preferences for portraits, food, landscapes, and more
- **Action Shot** — Automatically adjusts shutter speed for sharp, blur-free images of fast-moving subjects
- **Group Shot** — Blends multiple frames to guarantee no one's caught mid-blink
- **Photo Unblur** — Rescue old, slightly out-of-focus shots (works on photos from any phone)
- **Magic Eraser & Camouflage** — Remove distracting people or objects from any scene
- **Photo Enhancement Engine** — Brighter highlights, sharper details, better dynamic range

One honest note: reviewers of the 2025 model flagged that **portrait mode and low-light ultrawide performance** aren't quite at flagship-tier. There's also **no telephoto lens**, which limits optical zoom compared to the Galaxy S25 Ultra or Google Pixel 9 Pro. If zoom photography is your thing, the Razr Ultra 2026 will ask you to make a compromise.

## Moto AI: Promising, But Still a Work in Progress

Motorola has built an entire ecosystem of AI features around the Razr Ultra, anchored by a **dedicated AI key** on the side of the phone. Tap it, and you're dropped into a suite that blends **Google Gemini**, **Perplexity AI**, **Meta Llama**, and **Microsoft Copilot** into one unified experience.

The standout features include:

| Feature | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Look & Talk** | Glance at the cover screen to unlock + start an AI conversation |
| **Catch Me Up** | AI-summarizes all your notifications in one tap |
| **Pay Attention** | On-device voice transcription and summarization |
| **Remember This** | Saves personal context so AI can recall details later |
| **Go Live with Gemini** | Real-time AI conversation for brainstorming and prep |
| **Image Studio** | AI-generated images directly on device |
| **Playlist Studio** | AI-curated music playlists (Amazon Music integration) |

The honest verdict from a year of real-world use of the 2025 model? **Moto AI is interesting but inconsistent.** Features like Catch Me Up and Pay Attention are genuinely useful. Others feel half-baked. Motorola has a year of feedback to act on — and signs suggest the 2026 model will deliver a more polished version of these tools.
